What Data Science Is And What A Data Scientist Does

What is Data Science and what does a Data Scientist Do?

Now you might know data science is a very exciting career, you might understand it’s high in demand and even sexy… but maybe you still don’t know exactly what it is and that’s ok you’re not alone! With no standard definition around, Data Science is a profession which has attracted a lot of mystery about what it really is, daily tasks and their achievements. Then there are roles like data analyst and data engineer which are confused with what Data Scientists do but let me assure you they are quite different. Today I’m going to cover what exactly Data Science is, what data scientists do and what skills are needed to become a successful data scientist. There is so much data being constantly collected for businesses to perform better. Nonetheless, gathering data is the only first step; the data is useless until we do something with it. This is where data science comes in.

What is Data Science?

Data science is the art of taking data and transforming it into insightful information which enables us to use it to achieve desired results. It is an interdisciplinary field which takes from statistics, probability, computer science, and analytics. As data becomes readily available data science gets applied in more and more aspects of business. Four fields that make for the fundamental areas of knowledge to be successful in Data Science are Communication, Mathematics, Business and Computer science. These are the primary fields that you need to have a study background in or experience. Nonetheless, there is hardly anyone who is an expert at all four. A data scientist would typically do this through his business domain expertise, effective communications, results analysis by using different programming languages, software packages, data infrastructure, and statistical techniques.

Let us understand the goals and deliverables of a Data Scientist.

Here is a list of activities done by data scientists to achieve their final goal. They predict based on all the inputs, they classify data into spam or not spam category, they make recommendations (imagine Netflix), they detect pattern and group, anomaly detection, recognition of text, facial, image, etc., actionable insights from dashboards or reports, decision-making, scoring and ranking like FICO score, segmenting, optimization for risk management and finally forecasts as sales and returns. Each step acknowledges a goal or solves a problem to reach the ideal result.

What skills are needed to become a successful Data Scientist?

Programming knowledge — Coding is used when it comes to analyzing and processing data. Thus, programming competence is crucial in at least one such language. The more languages one knows, the better it is. Some popular are Python, R and SQL. Creative and Fast — it is required that a Data Scientist be creative in his approach to look at the problems in new light since they need to design algorithms that are fast and efficient. Their role not only revolves around why it should be done but also how it should be done. Be Curious — Always be ready to explore a new angle, curiosity leads Data Scientists to seek interesting patterns in large sets of information. Adequacy for Math — Since a major part of the job is to churn out raw data. The ability to deal with numbers is a must. Be decisive — Working day in and day out with data and information can get tiring and frustrating. Your decision needs to be firm to take the ordeals of this career and to also reap the benefits of it. Other skills like having analytical ability, good attention to detail, and have a sharp focus always helps Data Scientists to succeed in their career. This industry requires constant probabilistic thinking and how it is applied in day-to-day decisions. If you find yourself doing calculations, analyzing human behavior and choices you have to make on a daily basis, then the probability of this career choice being right for you is high. Your job would be to discover exciting and interesting information.
